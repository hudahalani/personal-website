Circuit Building - Electrical Engineering Labs

ECE110 - Electric Fields and Circuits

Course Overview

Participated in comprehensive hands-on circuit building labs throughout electrical engineering coursework. Gained practical experience in circuit design, analysis, and troubleshooting using professional laboratory equipment.

First Year Experience

  • Basic Circuits: Built and analyzed resistors, capacitors, and inductors
  • Lab Equipment: Digital multimeters, oscilloscopes, power supplies
  • Protoboard Assembly: Circuit construction and component placement
  • Safety Protocols: Electrical safety and proper lab procedures

Second Year Advanced Topics

  • Circuit Analysis: Kirchhoff's laws, Thevenin/Norton equivalents
  • AC Analysis: Phasors, frequency response, complex impedance
  • Filter Design: Low-pass, high-pass, and band-pass characteristics
  • Power Systems: Power distribution and analysis

Electronic Components (ECE231)

  • Semiconductor Devices: Diodes, BJTs, and MOSFETs
  • Amplifier Design: Common-emitter and common-source configurations
  • DC Biasing: Transistor biasing for amplifier operation
  • Small-Signal Models: Linear approximation techniques

Software Tools

LTSpice simulation, MATLAB analysis, LabVIEW data acquisition, and CAD tools for circuit design and PCB layout.

Skills Developed

Circuit design, problem solving, measurement techniques, documentation, team collaboration, and safety awareness. Formed foundation for embedded systems, FPGA programming, and software development projects.
